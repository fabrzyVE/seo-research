## Software Tools

### SeoGets

- Data Expansion: It pulls up to 50,000 rows of data via the GSC API (50x more than the standard GSC web interface).

- Portfolio Management: It allows SEO agencies and site owners to view multiple websites in a single, unified "Master Dashboard" rather than clicking through individual properties.

- Data Retention: It offers extended historical storage (up to 5 years), whereas GSC typically limits your look-back period

### Screaming Frog 

- Technical Audit: It is the industry standard for finding "under-the-hood" technical SEO issues.

- Simulation: It "crawls" a website exactly like a search engine would, reporting back on everything it finds (or everything it can't find).

### Ahrefs 

- Competitive Intelligence: Its primary strength is "spying" on your competitors. You can plug in any URL and see exactly which keywords they rank for and who is linking to them.

- Off-Page SEO: It is the industry standard for Link Building and backlink analysis.

- Web-Scale Data: It uses its own "AhrefsBot" to constantly map out the relationship between different websites across the globe.

## Key Terminology

- Impressions : when your website is viewed on the SERP but not clicked on 

- Bounce Rate : when your website gets a hit but the user leaves not long after 

- Click Through Rate (CTR) : how many people clicked your result on the search page [formula = clicks / impressions]

- Search Intent : users intentions to click your site (Transactional) / (Informational) / (Navigational)

- Backlinks & Internal Links : Backlinks is a link referencing to your site, and its the opposite for Internal Links 

- Canonical Tag : is the master link for a page that may be found on other pages of a site 

## Funnel Notes from Gemini 

In SEO, the "Funnel" is a way to visualize the journey a stranger takes from having a random problem to becoming your customer. Each stage requires a different type of content because the user's intent changes as they move down.

Here is a deep dive into each stage for your notes:

### 1. The Pre-Funnel (Engagement / Foundation)

This is often the "Technical" stage. Before a human ever sees your site, search engines (like Google) must be able to "engage" with it.

    User State: Unaware you exist; potentially not even searching yet.

    SEO Goal: Technical Health. If your site isn't "crawlable" or "indexable," the rest of the funnel doesn't matter.

    Focus: Site speed (Core Web Vitals), mobile-friendliness, and a clear site structure.

    Analogy: Building a store. If the front door is locked (technical error), no one can come in to see the products.

### 2. Top of Funnel (TOFU - Awareness)

This is the widest part of the funnel. The user has a problem or a curiosity but doesn't know the "solution" yet.

    User Intent: "I want to learn something."

    Search Examples: "Why is my grass turning brown?" or "How to start a garden."

    Content Type: Educational blog posts, "How-to" guides, infographics, and videos.

    Metric to Watch: Impressions and New Visitors. You are casting a wide net here.

    The Strategy: Don't sell your product yet. Just be the helpful expert who solves their immediate problem for free.

### 3. Middle of Funnel (MOFU - Consideration)

The user now knows what their problem is and is looking at different ways to fix it. They are "window shopping" for solutions.

    User Intent: "I'm comparing my options."

Search Examples: "Liquid vs. Granular fertilizer" or "Best lawn care services near me."

Content Type: Comparison guides, Case Studies, "Top 10" lists, and webinars.

Metric to Watch: Dwell Time and Returning Visitors.

The Strategy: Position your brand as the best solution by showing why you are different/better than the alternatives.

### 4. Bottom of Funnel (BOFU - Decision)

The user is ready to pull out their credit card. They just need one final "nudge" to choose you over a competitor.

    User Intent: "I am ready to buy now."

    Search Examples: "Blue Widget Co discount code" or "Buy heavy duty fertilizer online."

    Content Type: Product pages, pricing charts, free trials, and customer testimonials.

Metric to Watch: Conversion Rate and Branded Clicks.

The Strategy: Remove all friction. Make the "Buy" button easy to find and provide "Social Proof" (reviews) to build final trust.
